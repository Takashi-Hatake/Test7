Conventional System

A Conventional Fire Alarm System is a type of fire detection and alarm system that uses physical wiring to connect multiple devicesâ€”such as smoke detectors, heat detectors, and manual call pointsâ€”to a central control panel. It is one of the oldest and simplest forms of fire alarm systems.


Addressable System

An Addressable Fire Alarm System is a more advanced type of fire detection system in which each device (smoke detector, heat detector, call point, etc.) has a unique address or ID. This allows the fire alarm control panel to identify exactly which device has been activated, enabling faster and more precise response to potential fires.


Wireless System

A Wireless Fire Alarm System is a fire detection and alarm system where all devices (like smoke detectors, heat detectors, manual call points, and sounders) communicate with the control panel using wireless radio signals instead of wired connections.


Hybrid System

A Hybrid Fire Alarm System is a fire detection system that combines both wired and wireless technologies. It allows for wired devices where cabling is feasible and wireless devices in areas that are difficult or expensive to wire. This provides flexibility, scalability, and cost-effectiveness in a single system.

Maintenance & Testing


Maintaining accurate and timely log documentation for inspections, battery checks, and sensor calibrations is not just good practiceâ€”itâ€™s often a legal, safety, and operational requirement.
Regular inspection of a fire alarm system is crucial for ensuring the safety and reliability of the system in the event of a fire. These inspections are typically required by national fire codes (e.g., NFPA 72 in the United States), insurance companies, and local authorities.
Battery checks are a critical part of fire alarm system maintenance. Backup batteries ensure the system continues to function during a power outage.
Sensor calibration ensures that fire detection devices (especially smoke and heat detectors) respond accurately to fire conditions and not to false triggers (e.g., dust, steam). Regular calibration is vital to maintain system sensitivity, code compliance, and occupant safety.

Fire Prevented due to early Detection

Incident: A sawmill in the United States experienced a significant fire that led to a complete shutdown, resulting in damages amounting to $3.42 million.

Action Taken: In response, the company partnered with Infrared Cameras Inc. (ICI) to implement an early fire detection system using thermal imaging cameras and AI-driven software.

Fire Due to Lack of Maintenance

Incident: In 1989, a catastrophic explosion occurred at the Phillips Petroleum Company's Houston Chemical Complex in Pasadena, Texas, resulting in 23 fatalities and 314 injuries.

Cause: The explosion was attributed to the release of flammable process gases, which were ignited due to inadequate maintenance practices, including lack of process hazard analysis, insufficient standard operating procedures, and failure to maintain fire protection systems.

Consequences: The incident led to significant loss of life and property, highlighting the critical importance of regular maintenance and adherence to safety protocols in preventing such disasters.
